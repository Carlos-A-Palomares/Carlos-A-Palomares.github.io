Stone Enclosure: Rock Rings (1977-78) by Nancy Holt


Nancy Holt's "Stone Enclosure: Rock Rings" (1977-78) is an evocative artwork located at Western Washington University. This sculpture is a prime example of Holt's integration of celestial dynamics within a terrestrial context. Built with large stones sourced from the Brown Mountain near Harrison Hot Springs, B.C., the structure invites viewers to consider their place within the broader cosmos. The stones, aged over 230 million years, form two concentric circles that are aligned with the North Star, offering a space that not only reflects but interacts with the Earth's rotation.
