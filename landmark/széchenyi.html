Széchenyi Thermal Bath, Budapest, Hungary

Széchenyi Thermal Bath, located in Budapest, Hungary, is one of the largest spa complexes in Europe. Built in 1913 in the Neo-baroque style, it was later expanded in 1927 to include additional pools. The Széchenyi is famous for its grand architecture and the medicinal properties of its hot springs, which supply water to its numerous pools, including three outdoor and fifteen indoor. Offering a variety of spa services, it is a hub of relaxation and recreation, reflecting the spa culture that is an integral part of Hungarian tradition. The bath provides a unique experience of basking in outdoor pools even during the snowy winter months, attracting locals and tourists alike.
