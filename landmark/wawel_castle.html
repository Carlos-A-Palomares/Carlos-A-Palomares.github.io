Wawel Castle, Kraków, Poland

<

Wawel Castle, located on Wawel Hill in Kraków, is an architectural complex erected over many centuries and a symbol of Polish national identity. It has been the residence of kings, and the site of cultural and political life since at least the 11th century. The castle complex includes a number of structures from different periods, including the Renaissance courtyard and the Gothic cathedral where Polish monarchs were crowned and buried. Wawel Castle's architecture and art collections make it a crucial repository of Polish artistic and cultural heritage. Today, it serves as one of Poland's premier art museums and a major tourist attraction, offering insight into the rich history of the Polish state and its royal heritage.
