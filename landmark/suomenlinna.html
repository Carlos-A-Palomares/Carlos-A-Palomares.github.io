Suomenlinna, Helsinki, Finland

Suomenlinna, built during the mid-18th century, is a sea fortress that spans several islands in the archipelago near Helsinki, Finland. Originally constructed by the Swedes as Sveaborg to counter the maritime ambitions of the Russian Empire, it later played roles under Russian control and eventually in independent Finland. This UNESCO World Heritage site is appreciated not only for its historical military architecture but also as a popular park enjoyed by both locals and tourists. Its museums, restaurants, and theaters make Suomenlinna a vibrant cultural site, blending its storied past with contemporary Finnish culture.
