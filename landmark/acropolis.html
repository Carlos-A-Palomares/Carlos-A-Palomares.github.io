Acropolis of Athens, Greece

The Acropolis of Athens, built in the 5th century BCE, stands as a symbol of the artistic and political achievements of ancient Greece. Situated on a rocky hill above Athens, it includes several significant buildings, with the Parthenon being the most iconic. This site, dedicated to Athena, the patron goddess of the city, embodies the origins of democratic philosophy and classical art. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, the Acropolis attracts millions of visitors yearly, highlighting its enduring appeal and historical importance.
